## What You're Dealing With

The business email runs on a free personal address. The domain was registered years ago by someone who has since left, and nobody knows the password. Every new hire means a day of guessing which accounts they need and who can grant them. Nothing is written down, and every small technical problem turns into an afternoon of searching forums.

## What You Get

A tidy technology setup that the business actually owns. Professional email on your own domain, accounts registered to the company instead of to whoever set them up, devices configured consistently, and sensible security in place without anyone needing a lecture. All of it documented, so the next hire, and the next problem, takes minutes instead of a day.

## What We Take Care Of

- **Email**: professional addresses on your own domain, through Google Workspace or Microsoft 365
- **Domains**: registered in the business's name, renewing automatically, with access you control
- **Accounts**: the business owns every account, with shared access handled properly instead of one person's password on a sticky note
- **Devices**: computers, phones, and printers set up consistently and ready for whoever uses them
- **Security basics**: two-factor authentication, a password manager, and account recovery that works when someone is locked out
- **Documentation**: what exists, where it lives, and how to get in, written down in plain English

## How It Works

{{< steps >}}

1. **Inventory**: we map every account, domain, device, and subscription the business touches
2. **Fix**: we transfer ownership, close gaps, and set up what is missing
3. **Document**: you get a plain-English record of the whole setup and how to access it
4. **Support**: when something breaks or someone new joins, you have one place to call
